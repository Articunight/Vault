All drugs have myelosuppression

### Alkylating agents(6)
Cross-links DNA by binding to N7 guanine of DNA.
All are CCNS
Keto inactive, aldehyde and hydro active
Liver friendly as its metabolites are non-toxic
##### Busulfan
###### Clinical use
- Bone marrow ablation
###### Adverse effects
- Severe myelosuppression, pulmonary fibrosis
##### Nitrogen Mustards(2)
Cyclophosphamides, ifosfamide
Acrolein is the metabolite through which these act. Adverse on bladder
###### Clinical use
- Solid tumors, leukemia, lymphomas, SLE
###### Adverse effects
- SIADH, Fanconi syndrome, hemorrhagic cystitis and bladder cancer(prevent with mesna)

##### Nitrosoureas(2)
Carmustine, Iomustine 
###### Clinical use
- Brain tumors
###### Adverse effects
- Most Neurotoxic, convulsions dizziness ataxia

##### Procarbazine
###### Clinical use
- Hodgkin lymphoma
###### Adverse effects
- Pulmonary toxicity, Leukemia, disulfiram-like reaction
### Antimetabolites
S-phase specific except cladribine which is CCNS(Katzung says its CCS)
##### Thiopurines(2)
Azathioprine, *6-mercaptopurine*.
Purine (thiol) analogs that are activated by HGPRT
###### Clinical use
- Rheumatoid arthritis, IBD, SLE
###### Adverse effects
- GI, Liver toxicity. Inactivated by Xanthine oxidase, so gout drugs(Allopurinol, Febuxostat) can increase toxicity.
##### Purine analogs(2)
*Cladribine*, Pentostatin
###### Clinical use
- Hairy cell leukemia
###### Adverse effects
- Myelosuppression
##### Pyrimidine Analogs(2)
Cytarabine(DNA polymerase inhibitor) *5-fluorouracil*(thymidylate synthase inhibitor)
###### Clinical use
- Colon cancer, Pancreatic cancer, Basal cell carcinoma
###### Adverse effects
- Palmar-plantar erythrodysesthesia (hand-foot syndrome) Life

##### Hydroxyurea
Inhibitor of ribonucleotide reductase
###### Clinical use
- Myeloproliferative disorders(polycythemia vera) sickle cell anemia
###### Adverse effects
- Megaloblastic anemia
##### Methotrexate
Folic acid analog inhibiting DHFR(dihydrofolate reductase)
###### Clinical use
- All leukemias, lymphomas, choriocarcinoma, sarcoma
- Ectopic pregnancy, medical abortion, rheumatoid arthritis, IBD
###### Adverse effects
- Myelosuppression reversible with leucovorin
- Hepatotoxicity, mucositis
- Pulmonary fibrosis, folate deficiency, nephrotoxicity
### Antitumor antibiotics
All CCNS except bleomycin which is G2/M
Bleomycin induces superoxide formation->breaks DNA strands
Dactinomycin intercalates into DNA
Anthracyclines generate free radicals and also inhibits topoisomerase 2
##### Bleomycin
###### Clinical use
- Testicular cancer, Hodgkin lymphoma
###### Adverse effects
- Pulmonary fibrosis, skin hyperpigmentation
##### Dactinomycin
###### Clinical use
- Wilms tumor, Ewing sarcoma, 
###### Adverse effects
- Myelosuppression
##### Anthracyclines(2)
Doxorubicin, daunorubicin
topoisomerase 2 inhibitor
###### Clinical use
- Solid tumors, leukemia, lymphoma
###### Adverse effects
- Dilated cardiomyopathy(irreversible), myelosuppression

### Miscellaneous anticancer drugs
#### Platinum compounds(3)
Cisplatin, carboplatin, oxaliplatin
Cross-link DNA, CCNS
###### Clinical use
- Solid tumors(*testicular*, bladder, ovarian, gi, lung) and lymphomas
###### Adverse effects
- Nephrotoxicity(Fanconi syndrome) peripheral neuropathy, ototoxicity

#### Microtubule inhibitors(2)
M-phase specific. Stops at metaphase
Prevent MT spindle breakdown
##### Vinca alkaloids(2)
Vincristine, vinblastine
Bind b-tubulin and inhibit polymerization
###### Clinical use
- Solid tumors, leukemias, Hodgkin and non-hodgkins lymphomas
###### Adverse effects
- Vin*cris*tine *crisps* the nerves(Neurotoxic, alopecia) Vin*blast*ine *blasts* the marrow(myelosuppression)
##### *Tax*anes(2)
Docetaxel, paclitaxel
Hyper*stabilizes* polymerized microtubules preventing breakdown
Taxes stabilize
###### Clinical use
- Ovarian, Breast carcinomas
###### Adverse effects
-  Neuropathy, hypersensitivity
